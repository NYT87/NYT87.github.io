## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are people evaluating software development companies for product, platform, or infrastructure work. They are comparing credibility, technical depth, delivery maturity, and fit before deciding whether to reach out.

The job to be done is to quickly understand what NYT87 builds, how it works, and whether it feels like a trustworthy technical partner for serious software.

### Brand Personality
The brand should feel formal, geeky, and smart.

The interface should communicate rigor, technical clarity, and calm confidence rather than hype. It should feel like a strong engineering studio, not a generic startup landing page.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a polished editorial-technical direction: structured layouts, disciplined typography, restrained motion, and a warm but precise palette. The visual system should feel intentional and intelligent, with subtle system-console and architectural-document cues rather than glassy SaaS tropes.

Support both light and dark themes through system preference. Avoid generic AI-era startup aesthetics such as hero metric cards, decorative glassmorphism, repeated lifted cards, and flashy gradients.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with technical credibility over marketing volume.
2. Prefer structure, hierarchy, and clarity over decorative surfaces.
3. Make interactions accessible and explicit, especially for keyboard and mobile users.
4. Use a consistent tokenized visual system that works in both light and dark modes.
5. Let the site feel quietly distinctive through typography, composition, and detail rather than trend-driven effects.
